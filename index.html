Similarly, machine-generated recipes are not out of the question, and one can develop computer programs that can float novel recipes rich in taste and nutritional values. The major hurdle in such developments is the lack of resources in machine-readable ingredients, instructions, and nutritional values. Evolution and Evolutionary Algorithms

Evolutionary algorithms are algorithms that are based on species evolution; in general, they are based on Charles Darwin's basic evolutionary theory. The implementation of evolutionary mechanisms varies considerably; yet, the essential concept behind all of these variants is the same. The presence of a population of individuals exposed to environmental pressure is defined by evolutionary algorithms, which leads to natural selection, i.e. the survival of the fittest, and, as a result, an increase in the population's average fitness. Fitness is a statistic for how well an organism adapts to its surroundings; the higher the fitness, the more fit and adaptable the organism is to its surroundings. In general, evolutionary algorithms only consider a subset of mechanisms that have evolved during the span of biological evolution.
